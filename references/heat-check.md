# Heat Check

Use this reference when the user asks whether a topic is hot, current, timely, suitable for chasing, related to hot searches, or worth writing now.

## Safety Boundary

Use public and permitted information only. Do not build crawlers, bypass login, solve CAPTCHAs, evade rate limits, automate platform accounts, or claim access to hidden trend data.

Acceptable sources:

- Web search results with publication dates.
- Official or public trend tools such as Baidu Index, WeChat Index, Douyin Hotspot/巨量算数, Bilibili charts, Weibo hot search pages, Zhihu hot list, Toutiao hot list, and public media pages.
- Public reputable media reports.
- User-provided account analytics, platform screenshots, keyword lists, or data exports.

If a source cannot be accessed directly, state the limitation and use available public signals.

## When To Search

Use web search before judging heat when the request includes:

- 当下, 最近, 今天, 本周, 近期, 现在
- 热搜, 热点, 爆款, 趋势, 风口
- platform names such as 微信, 微博, 抖音, 小红书, B站, 知乎, 百度
- recent facts, product releases, regulations, public events, celebrity/company/person claims, prices, rankings, or statistics

For evergreen strategy topics, search only if current facts affect the recommendation.

## Heat Signals

Judge heat from multiple signals instead of one list.

### Recent Discussion

Look for fresh articles, posts, news, videos, Q&A, and search results. Prefer sources from the last 7-30 days for fast-moving topics.

### Search Trend

Use search index or visible search-result freshness when available. A rising keyword is stronger than an old high-volume keyword.

### Cross-Platform Spread

Check whether the topic appears across different content environments:

- WeChat/public account or search ecosystem.
- Search engines.
- Q&A/community platforms.
- Short-video or social platforms.
- News/media.

Cross-platform spread means the topic is more likely to be a public issue rather than a one-platform joke.

### WeChat Fit

A topic is not automatically good for公众号 just because it is hot. Check whether it can become:

- a reasoned article
- a practical guide
- an opinion essay
- a case analysis
- a reader decision framework
- a series topic

### Content Vacancy

Check whether existing discussion is repetitive. A topic with heavy coverage can still be worth writing if there is a clear vacancy:

- ordinary-reader perspective
- industry insider perspective
- counterintuitive view
- practical method
- local or niche case
- consequence analysis
- mistake/pitfall summary

## Heat Score

Use this 100-point score:

- Recent discussion: 25
- Search trend or freshness: 20
- Cross-platform spread: 20
- WeChat ecosystem relevance: 15
- Content vacancy: 10
- Account fit: 10

Bands:

- 85-100: Hot and worth acting on now.
- 70-84: Warm or rising. Worth writing if the angle is strong.
- 50-69: Mild heat. Better as evergreen or niche content.
- Below 50: Not a heat-driven topic. Write only if account fit is strong.

## Chasing Decision

Recommend "write now" only when:

- The topic has enough heat or timely decision value.
- The account has a credible angle.
- There is a content vacancy.
- The article can be completed with verified material.

Recommend "do not chase" when:

- The topic is hot but unrelated to the account.
- Discussion is already saturated and the user has no new angle.
- The topic requires unverifiable claims.
- The controversy is high but value to readers is low.
- The topic will age before the article can be written.
