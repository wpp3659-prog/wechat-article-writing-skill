# Writing Style

Write like a real public-account editor: opinionated, useful, slightly imperfect, and easy to read on a phone.

## Opening

The first 3 sentences must create one of these effects:

- Pain: name a familiar frustration.
- Conflict: challenge a common belief.
- Suspense: show an unusual result before explaining it.
- Scene: put the reader in a concrete moment.
- Promise: tell the reader what they will gain.

Never open with broad background such as "随着时代的发展" or "在当今社会".

## Rhythm

Use mobile-friendly paragraphs.

- Most paragraphs: 20-80 Chinese characters.
- Occasional one-line paragraph for emphasis.
- Avoid long block paragraphs above 150 Chinese characters.
- Mix short and medium sentences.
- Do not make every section follow the same internal pattern.

## Human Texture

Add real-feeling texture without inventing facts:

- Use concrete scenarios: "你晚上十一点打开电脑..." rather than "很多人".
- Use measured uncertainty: "我的判断是", "至少在这个场景里".
- Use light self-correction when natural: "更准确地说..."
- Allow one small aside if it helps the rhythm.
- Use editor notes when personal experience is needed but unavailable.

Example editor note:

```markdown
<!-- 编辑建议：这里替换成你自己的真实经历，会让文章更像本人写的。 -->
```

## Language To Avoid

Avoid these terms unless quoting or rewriting user-provided text:

- 首先, 其次, 再者, 最后, 总之, 综上所述, 总而言之.
- 在当今时代, 随着社会的发展, 值得注意的是, 不可否认, 众所周知.
- 非常重要, 至关重要, 具有重要意义, 引发广泛关注.
- 未来可期, 让我们拭目以待, 愿你我都能.

Prefer:

- "说白了"
- "问题在于"
- "更麻烦的是"
- "这件事真正值得警惕的地方是"
- "如果只看表面，很容易误判"

## Detail Rules

Use specific details only when they are true, sourced, provided, or clearly hypothetical.

Allowed:

- "假设你是一个刚开始做账号的人..."
- "比如，一个团队在做选题时..."
- "你可能会遇到这样的场景..."

Not allowed:

- "我认识一个朋友..." unless the user provided that story.
- Specific numbers without a source.
- Named companies, policies, dates, or expert claims without verification.

## Ending

Choose a natural ending:

- Practical article: one clear next step.
- Opinion article: one firm judgment plus discussion question.
- Story article: return to the opening image.
- Reflection article: leave a quiet aftertaste.
- Product article: invite a specific action.

Do not end with a stiff recap of every section.
