# Topic Evaluation Rubric

Use this rubric when ranking public-account topic ideas. Score honestly. Do not inflate scores to make weak ideas look publishable.

## 100-Point Score

### Reader Relevance - 15

- 13-15: Clear target reader with a real, recognizable need.
- 8-12: Reader is plausible but broad.
- 0-7: No clear reader or only a generic public audience.

### Pain Or Benefit Strength - 15

- 13-15: The topic touches urgent pain, strong desire, meaningful risk, or clear gain.
- 8-12: The topic has moderate interest but weak urgency.
- 0-7: The topic is informational only and easy to ignore.

### Distinct Angle - 15

- 13-15: Has a sharp judgment, fresh framing, or counterintuitive insight.
- 8-12: Useful but familiar.
- 0-7: Generic category, slogan, or recycled angle.

### WeChat Article Suitability - 15

- 13-15: Can support a 1500-3000 character article with structure, examples, and emotional or practical payoff.
- 8-12: Better as a short post but can become an article with work.
- 0-7: Too thin, too visual, too newsy, or too fragmented for a public-account article.

### Evidence And Material Support - 15

- 13-15: Has user material, public facts, cases, examples, or lived scenes to support it.
- 8-12: Can be written with common reasoning but needs more concrete support.
- 0-7: Requires facts, cases, or claims that are missing or unverifiable.

### Account Fit - 10

- 9-10: Strongly matches the account's reader, voice, domain, and long-term positioning.
- 5-8: Related but not central.
- 0-4: Hot or interesting, but off-position.

### Timeliness Or Heat - 10

- 9-10: Current public discussion, rising interest, or timely decision need.
- 5-8: Evergreen with some current relevance.
- 0-4: No timing advantage.

For evergreen topics, do not punish the score heavily for low heat if reader relevance, pain, and article quality are strong. Mark the topic as "evergreen" instead of "hot".

### Title Potential - 5

- 5: Can produce a concrete, non-clickbait title with tension.
- 3-4: Title can work but needs sharpening.
- 0-2: Title likely stays broad or dull.

## Recommendation Bands

- 90-100: Strong topic. Recommend writing now if evidence is available.
- 80-89: Good topic. Recommend after sharpening the angle or adding material.
- 70-79: Usable topic. Keep in topic pool, but do not make it the first choice.
- 60-69: Weak topic. Rewrite the angle before outlining.
- Below 60: Reject or merge into another topic.

## Hard-Fail Checks

Reject or pause the topic when any item is true:

- The topic depends on recent facts but no source can be verified.
- The angle requires fake personal experience, fake cases, or invented data.
- The title promise cannot be delivered by the available material.
- The topic is mainly a hot keyword with no account fit.
- The topic is likely to mislead readers on medical, legal, financial, or other high-stakes decisions.
- The topic attacks a person or group without evidence and public-interest value.
- The topic cannot support more than a few paragraphs of useful content.

## Ranking Method

When ranking multiple topics:

1. Score each topic with the 100-point rubric.
2. Mark the topic type: hot, evergreen, series, conversion, opinion, practical guide, story, or analysis.
3. Select top recommendations for different strategic purposes when useful:
   - Best for immediate traffic.
   - Best for account positioning.
   - Best for trust building.
   - Best for conversion.
   - Best for series expansion.
4. Recommend one main topic unless the user asks for a large topic pool.

## Output Format

Use this format when scoring topics:

```markdown
| 选题 | 总分 | 类型 | 强项 | 主要风险 | 建议 |
|---|---:|---|---|---|---|
```

For the selected topic:

```markdown

- 选题：
- 总分：
- 为什么推荐：
- 需要补充的材料：
- 不建议采用的写法：
- 更好的切口：
```
